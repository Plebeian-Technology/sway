Context: Baltimore City Code

Summary:
FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Finance to conduct a disparity study to determine 5 whether a disparity exists between the number of available businesses that are owned by 6 returning citizens, or whose employees are comprised of 50% or more returning citizens, 7 providing goods or services in specified business categories and the number who are 8 contracting with the City as a prime contractor or subcontractor. 9 Recitals 10 A returning citizen is an individual who was formally charged with a crime, convicted of a 11 crime, or participated in a diversion program where the individual received rehabilitative 12 instruction and support in lieu of a criminal conviction. 13 According to the National League of Cities, an estimated 600,000 citizens per year return to 14 the community. The unemployment rate is 27.3% for returning citizens who were formerly 15 incarcerated, versus 5.8% in the general population. Returning citizens often experience unique 16 barriers to employment and long term job security, including lack of formal education, stigma, 17 and employers that use criminal background checks as a screening tool for trustworthiness. 18 According to the Wharton School of Business at the University of Pennsylvania, 19 entrepreneurship often leads returning citizens to higher incomes than traditional employment, as 20 well as lower rates of recidivism, even when compared with their peers who pursue traditional 21 employment. Previously incarcerated individuals are 5% more likely to start their own business, 22 compared with the general public. Entrepreneurship creates a pathway to stable income while 23 also assisting returning citizens with avoiding discrimination and inequality in the traditional 24 labor market. 25 Given the unique opportunities that entrepreneurship creates for returning citizens, the City of 26 Baltimore has an opportunity to support the City’s community of returning citizens by improving 27 its procurement practices and including equitable consideration of returning citizen-owned and 28 employing business enterprises (RCOEBE).
