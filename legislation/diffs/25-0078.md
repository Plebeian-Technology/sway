21 SECTION 2.  AND BE IT FURTHER ORDAINED, That the proceedings for the condemnation and
22 closing of portions of certain streets and alleys bounded by Riggs Avenue, N. Payson Street, the
23 former bed of Mosher Street and the Land of National Railroad Passenger Corporation (Amtrak)
24 and the rights of all interested parties shall be regulated by and in accordance with all applicable
25 provisions of state and local law and with all applicable rules and regulations.
26 SECTION 3.  AND BE IT FURTHER ORDAINED, That after the closing under this Ordinance, all
27 subsurface structures and appurtenances now owned by the Mayor and City Council of Baltimore
28 continue to be the property of the Mayor and City Council, in fee simple, until their use has been
29 abandoned by the Mayor and City Council. If any person wants to remove, alter, or interfere with
30 them, that person must first obtain permission from the Mayor and City Council and, in the
31 application for this permission, must agree to pay all costs and expenses, of every kind, arising
32 out of the removal, alteration, or interference.
33 SECTION 4.  AND BE IT FURTHER ORDAINED, That no building or structure of any kind
34 (including but not limited to railroad tracks) may be constructed or erected in or on any part of
35 the street closed under this Ordinance until all subsurface structures and appurtenances owned by
36 the Mayor and City Council of Baltimore have been abandoned by the Mayor and City Council
37 or, at the expense of the person seeking to erect the building or structure, have been removed and
38 relaid in accordance with the specifications and under the direction of the Director of
39 Transportation of Baltimore City.
40 SECTION 5.  AND BE IT FURTHER ORDAINED, That after the closing under this Ordinance, all
41 subsurface structures and appurtenances owned by any person other than the Mayor and City
42 Council of Baltimore shall be removed by and at the expense of their owners, promptly upon
43 notice to do so from the Director of Transportation.
